ISEE Professional Development Program Participant, 2016

I participated the ISEE PDP at UCSC in 2016, where I learned inquiry-based teaching and designed an inquiry activity with three colleagues from UT Austin. The goal of this activity was to teach how selection biases in observations (e.g., flux-limited observations) can affect the interpretations of astronomical data. In this activity, students carried out their investigation in a real-world astronomy setting: they selected and asked the questions that motivated their study, and decided on spe- cific variables they wanted to change. In each case they would then predict the outcomes and propose for new observations while taking into account on limited resources. In the end they were able to provide detailed explanations given the evidence they gathered.

Active learning currirulum for Introductory Astronomy, 2012

An old webpage of this course.

I taught an undergraduate-level Introductory Astronomy course with Nichloas Lee at University of Hawai'i. We implimented techniques such as think-pair-share throughout the classes to assess students' prior knowledge and learning progress, and create opportunities that make their thinking visible. We also facillitated learning activities such as role-play and small group discussions where students can confront their misconceptions, brainstorm with peers, and eventually come to their own understanding.


Students supervised

I am really fortunated to work with many passionated undergraduate researchers on a variety of topics such as galaxy mergers and young galaxy clusters.

Richard Seifert (UT undergraduate) is using Scuba-2 data in the COSMOS field to characterize how galaxies' gas content relates to the environments they reside in (2016-present).

Derek Holman (2016 TAURUS scholar) studies the importance of galaxy mergers in a young, proto galaxy cluster at z~2.1 (2016-present).

Aaron Weiner studied UV-FIR SEDs of a sample of late-stage galaxy mergers compiled by cross-referencing Galaxy Zoo morphology classifications and IRAC faint source catalog (2014).
